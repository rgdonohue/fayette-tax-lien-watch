# Fayette County Tax Lien Certificate Purchasing: Research Brief
*Prepared for Christine, Director of Seedleaf — for presentation to Lexington Urban County Council*
*Data as of 2026-03-22 | All figures derived from Fayette County Clerk land records and delinquent tax bill system*

---

## Executive Summary

Third-party purchasing of delinquent tax certificates is a legal but consequential activity in
Fayette County. A single operator — **KY Lien Holdings, LLC** and affiliated entities, all
linked to Murray, Kentucky attorney **Justin H. Ramey** — has recorded at least **234 certificate
of delinquency assignments** across Fayette County since 2010, making it one of the largest
concentrated certificate purchasers in the county's public record.

This brief documents the operator's portfolio history, entity structure, and the broader landscape
of delinquent tax bills in Fayette County, using data drawn entirely from public records.

---

## Section 1: The Delinquent Tax Bill Landscape

The Fayette County delinquent tax system currently shows **6,317 unpurchased delinquent tax bills**
across tax years 2016–2024. These are bills that remain unresolved in the county's system.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total unresolved bills (2016–2024) | 6,317 |
| Median original tax debt | **$28.87** |
| Bills under $50 original debt | 4,115 (65.1%) |
| Bills under $100 original debt | 4,721 (74.7%) |
| Total original face value | $1,963,709 |
| Total currently owed (with penalties & interest) | $4,479,049 |
| Median multiplier (current owed ÷ original debt) | **3.0×** |
| Bills now more than 3× original amount | 3,171 |
| Bills now more than 10× original amount | 469 |

**Key finding:** The majority of delinquent tax bills in Fayette County are for very small
original debts — most under $50. Kentucky's 12% annual interest rate on purchased certificates
means these debts compound rapidly. A bill with a $29 original face value that goes unpurchased
or unresolved for several years can grow to $87–$290+ in total obligation.

## Section 4: Policy Context

### How the system works

Under KRS 134.128–134.452, counties sell delinquent property tax debts to registered
third-party purchasers at annual tax certificate sales. The purchaser:
1. Pays the delinquent tax amount to the county (owner receives no immediate benefit)
2. Earns 12% annual interest on the certificate
3. May enforce the lien by filing a foreclosure action after a 1-year tolling period
4. May sell/reassign the certificate to another party

### What this means for property owners

- The property owner's debt does not disappear — it transfers from the county to a private
  purchaser, often with less flexibility for resolution
- Interest accrues at 12% annually; a $100 original bill becomes $340+ after 10 years
- Foreclosure on a $29 tax debt is legally permitted under Kentucky law
- The 12% return is guaranteed by statute and effectively risk-free for the purchaser if the
  property has any equity value

### Scale of the small-debt problem in Fayette County

Of 6,317 current unresolved bills in Fayette County:
- 4,115 (65.1%) were for original debts under $50
- 4,721 (74.7%) were for original debts under $100
- These small debts are now collectively owed at 3× their original face value on median

## Section 5: Data Sources and Methodology

| Source | What it contains | How obtained |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| Fayette County Clerk land records (fayettedeeds.com) | All recorded CERT DEL ASSIGN, DEL TAX RELEASE, REASSIGN, and ASSIGN/ENCUMBRA instruments | Scraped via direct HTTP session replay, 2026-03-22 |
| Fayette County delinquent tax bill search (fayettedeeds.com) | Current unresolved delinquent bills, 2016–2024, with face value and amount due | Scraped 2025–2026 |
| KY DOR third-party purchaser lists | Annual registered purchasers by county | PDFs downloaded from revenue.ky.gov, years 2016, 2023, 2024, 2025, 2026 |

**Methodology notes:**
- "Active" status means no DEL TAX RELEASE or similar instrument found in public land records.
  This does not rule out informal resolutions or instruments not yet indexed by the clerk.
- Certificate-to-property matching uses instrument cross-references (book/page numbers).
  165 of 169 release instruments matched directly; 4 matched via fallback cross-reference.
- Entity name normalization collapses "KY Lien Holdings, LLC" and "KY Lien Holdings LLC"
  into a single canonical name.
- Land records search returns a maximum of ~999 records per query. Entity-specific queries
  (by grantee/grantor name) were used and verified complete by cross-referencing CSV record
  counts against source HTML files — all counts match exactly. A general county-wide year query
  for 2022 returned exactly 999 results (potential truncation for non-Ramey entities); a KORA
  request for the complete clerk index would resolve this for a comprehensive county view.
