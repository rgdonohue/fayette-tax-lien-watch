# Open Records Requests

## For Christine — How to File

**What to expect:** Each agency must respond in writing within 5 business days (KRS 61.880(1)).
That response may be "here are your records," "we need more time," or a fee estimate — any of
those is normal. If they quote a fee, let me know and we can decide whether to narrow the request.

**If you get a denial or no response after 5 business days**, let me know — you can appeal to
the KY Attorney General.

### Pending resubmissions (Requests 4 and 2)

Requests 4 and 2 were sent to `orr.custodian@ky.gov` on their first attempt. That address
routes to the Finance and Administration Cabinet (FAC) Office of General Counsel — not to
DOR's records office. FAC responded on 3/25/2026 (O-26-404, O-26-405) saying they don't
maintain these records and redirected us. See [Response Log](#response-log) below.

But the routing mistake was only part of the problem. Request 4 also conflated two different
record systems:

- **DOR records**: statewide third-party purchaser registration/certification under KRS 134.129 and 103 KAR 5:190.
- **Fayette Clerk records**: county-level sale registration packets, priority lists, current purchase lists, and sale records under 103 KAR 5:180.

To answer "who registered to buy Fayette certificates and what did they buy," we need both.
Request 4 has been split into **4a** (Fayette Clerk — county sale records) and **4b** (DOR —
statewide registration lists).

Request 2 (delinquency lists) is being resubmitted to the Clerk (**2a**) and to DOR (**2b**).
FAC's redirect to the Clerk doesn't prove DOR lacks the lists — KRS 134.131 requires clerks to
submit them to DOR. Parallel requests make sense.

### How to file

All requests below are sent by email:

1. Open a new email
2. Enter the **To** address and **Subject** line shown
3. Copy the **Body** text into the email
4. Replace `[YOUR EMAIL]` and `[YOUR PHONE]` with your contact info
5. Send

**Note on DOR:** Kentucky law does not require you to use any agency's particular form
(KRS 61.872). A letter or email is sufficient. Send DOR requests to **orr.revenue@ky.gov**.

**Send each request as a separate email** — even when multiple requests go to the same office.
This reduces the chance that a broad request delays a simpler one.

## Reference: Legal Basis

- **Authority:** Kentucky Open Records Act, KRS 61.870–61.884
- **Method:** Email, fax, or letter — agencies cannot require use of a particular form (KRS 61.872(2))
- **Residency:** Required (KRS 61.872(1)); Christine qualifies as a KY resident under KRS 61.870(10)(a)
- **Response deadline:** 5 business days for initial written response (KRS 61.880(1))
- **Fees:** Noncommercial copy fees cannot exceed actual cost of reproduction (KRS 61.874). DOR's standard paper fee is $0.10/page per their ORR page.
- **Appeal:** If denied, appeal to KY Attorney General. The AG has 20 business days to issue a decision after receiving the appeal (KRS 61.880(2)).
- **AG standardized form (optional):** https://www.ag.ky.gov/Documents/2021_Standardized_Open_Records_Request_Form_V3.pdf
- **FAC vs DOR contacts:** `orr.custodian@ky.gov` routes to FAC Office of General Counsel. For DOR-specific records, use `orr.revenue@ky.gov`. Do not rely on FAC to forward DOR requests.

### Key statutes and regulations for this project
- **KRS 134.129:** Third-party purchaser registration with DOR (statewide)
- **KRS 134.131:** County clerk submission of delinquency lists to DOR (effective May 15, 2013)
- **103 KAR 5:180:** County-level third-party sale procedures, including registration with county clerk
- **103 KAR 5:190:** DOR statewide registration/certification of third-party purchasers
