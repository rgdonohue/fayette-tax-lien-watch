# Open Records Requests

## For Christine — How to File

These are ready-to-send emails. For each request:

1. Open a new email
2. Enter the **To** address and **Subject** line shown
3. Copy the **Body** text into the email
4. Replace `[YOUR EMAIL]` and `[YOUR PHONE]` with your contact info
5. Send

You can send all five on the same day. Requests 1 and 3 go to the same office (Fayette
County Clerk) — you can send them as separate emails or combine into one, your choice.

**Start with Request 4** — it's the most time-sensitive for the council presentation.

**What to expect:** Each agency must respond in writing within 3 business days. That response
may be "here are your records" or "we need more time" — either is normal. If they quote a fee,
let me know and we can decide whether to narrow the request.

**If you get a denial or no response after 5 business days**, let me know — there's a formal
appeal process through the Attorney General.

## Reference: Legal Basis

- **Authority:** Kentucky Open Records Act, KRS 61.870–61.884
- **Method:** Email is permitted (KRS 61.872(2))
- **Residency:** Required (KRS 61.872(1)); Christine qualifies as a KY resident under KRS 61.870(10)(a)
- **Fees:** County clerks may charge up to $0.50/page; DOR averages ~$120 when fees apply (~20% of requests)
- **Appeal:** If denied, appeal to KY Attorney General within 20 business days
- **AG standardized form (optional):** https://www.ag.ky.gov/Documents/2021_Standardized_Open_Records_Request_Form_V3.pdf
